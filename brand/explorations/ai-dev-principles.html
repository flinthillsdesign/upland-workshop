Principles for Building AI Apps

A complex system that works is invariably found to have evolved from a simple system that worked. A complex system designed from scratch never works and cannot be patched up to make it work. You have to start over with a working simple system.

John Gall — The Systems Bible

Example: Project Scheduling

The Word document worked. The software version does the same job — but makes it easier, faster, and visual. Scheduling collisions are immediately obvious. Staffing is real FTE math instead of gut feel. Cash flow forecasts generate themselves from the same data you're already entering. The real unlock is what happens next: ODIN pulls this schedule data, merges it with project info, financials, and client history, and draws conclusions no single tool could reach on its own.

The fall gap for design persists even with potential projects factored in — pipeline conversion helps, but doesn't close it.

Fab drops to near-idle by late 2026, then partially recovers. Design thins out through spring 2027. The gaps hit each team at different times, but the cash flow impact compounds — sustained negative months from fall onward. Specific potential projects would bridge the worst of it, but only if they close.

Example: Meeting Agendas

ODIN generates weekly meeting agendas by pulling from Basecamp tasks, the project schedule, and team workload data. It identifies flags — overdue tasks, workload imbalances, scheduling conflicts — and surfaces them automatically. The agenda isn't a blank document someone fills out on Monday morning. It's a draft built from what the systems already know, reviewed and adjusted by a human before the meeting starts.

Goats — above-and-beyond work that wasn't on the list. When someone earns a goat in ODIN, it gets celebrated. Click to see what that looks like.

Example: Prioritization & Process

Tony is where the team defines process rules — "when X happens, we should do Y." These are the institutional knowledge that usually lives in someone's head or a forgotten wiki. ODIN pulls Tony's shoulds, maps them against every active project in Basecamp, and folds the results into a daily briefing alongside priorities, next steps, and predictions.

Daily Briefing

None of this existed as a single view before. Priorities were in someone's head, process steps were remembered or forgotten, and next steps came out of meetings that may or may not have covered everything. Now ODIN synthesizes across Tony's rules, Basecamp tasks, the schedule, and project data to surface what matters today — with names attached and actions suggested.

The Lesson: You Can't Skip the Middle

Quotes can generate a complete proposal from a sentence. The AI is accurate. But the team still reviews every line item. The technology leaped ahead — the trust didn't. A system that's 95% right still has to prove it 50 times before anyone stops checking.

You can't earn trust on the hard thing until you've earned it on the easy thing.

Don't launch with the most impressive feature. Launch with the most verifiable one.

Let people confirm it's right about easy things before you ask them to trust it on hard things.
